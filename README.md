## Case Study Executive Summary
**File:** `Case study carter ppt - GrpWork.pdf` (Executive Briefing Deck)

In fast-moving corporate environments, leadership teams are frequently forced to make high-impact choices using incomplete or skewed datasets. This project breaks down the mathematical and psychological trap of **Survivorship Bias** within Carter Racing's operations, proving how looking *only* at historical failures while ignoring baseline success conditions creates a false sense of security that can completely derail a company.

### Strategic Frameworks & Critical Core Analysis

#### 1. The Mathematical Trap of Survivorship Bias
* **The Fragmented Baseline:** Initial historical analysis highlighted 24 competitive entry logs with 7 engine gasket failures (a 29% baseline failure rate). When plotting engine failure events against ambient air temperature, no clear correlation was found within the observed 53°F–82°F range.
* **The Analytical Blind Spot:** Discovered a massive data boundary error: the dataset omitted the ambient temperatures of the **races that finished successfully without failure**. 
* **The Full Baseline Proof:** Integrating the missing data vectors reveals that gasket failure probabilities spike exponentially as temperatures drop below 50°F. Deciding to enter a race at an ambient temperature of 40°F without examining the full baseline introduces an unacceptable, unmanaged risk of structural failure.

#### 2. Corporate Risk Assessment & The BCG Matrix
* **Portfolio Vectoring:** Positioned the upcoming high-stakes Pocono International Raceway entry onto a **BCG Growth-Share Matrix** as a classic **Question Mark**. It presents massive potential for national TV exposure and multi-million-dollar sponsor acquisition ($2M Goodstone Oil contract), but carries extreme technical uncertainty and unverified reliability metrics.
* **Downstream Cost Valuation:** Quantified the catastrophic corporate impact of a public engine failure, including immediate asset destruction, long-term brand equity devaluation, sponsor withdrawal, and terminal cash-flow strain.

#### 3. Strategic Recommendations for High-Uncertainty Environments
* **Data Logging Protocols:** Establishes rigorous corporate controls requiring analysts to evaluate complete populations (both successes and failures) before clearing high-risk operational steps.
* **Decoupled Governance:** Introduces frameworks to separate commercial sponsorship pressures from engineering and technical safety approvals to eliminate confirmation bias during critical checkpoints.
